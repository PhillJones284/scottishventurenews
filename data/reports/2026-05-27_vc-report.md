# Scottish VC Intelligence Report — 27 May 2026

---

## 1. Executive Summary

**12 named investment deals** with high or medium confidence were captured in this run, representing an estimated **£39.7 million** in capital deployed into Scottish and Scotland-linked companies. This figure includes USD-denominated rounds converted at prevailing rates (Bioliberty $10.5m at approximately £7.78m; Launchpad $11m at approximately £8.15m).

**Headline observations:**

1. **Deep tech and cleantech are dominating the deal flow.** Five of the twelve named deals fall into Deep Tech, three into Energy & Cleantech, and three into Life Sciences. The Exergy3 £10m seed round — the largest individual deal in this dataset — sits at the intersection of both themes: a University of Edinburgh spinout converting surplus renewable electricity into industrial heat. It attracted three overseas investors (Paris, Munich, Singapore) alongside Scottish Enterprise, an unusual international syndicate for a Scottish seed deal.

2. **Tricapital is by far the most active investor by deal count this period.** The Edinburgh-based angel syndicate led four separate deals totalling over £2.75m in disclosed funding — HonuWorx, Kaly, Sisaltech, and JET Connectivity — all within the broad theme of industrial transition, ocean technology, and critical infrastructure. No other investor came close to that deal cadence in this run.

3. **PXN Ventures is asserting itself as Scotland's most prominent institutional lead.** Formed from the 2025 merger of Par Equity and Praetura Ventures, PXN led or joined three deals this period: the £6m Earth Blox Series A (lead), the £2.5m Quantcore seed (co-lead), and the Launchpad $11m Series A (strategic investor). Named on Sifted's 2026 list of the 50 best UK/Ireland investors, PXN is positioning itself as the go-to institutional backer for Scottish deeptech and climate companies.

**Notable caveats:** Four records in this dataset have no company name and are effectively ghost records — likely caused by the scraper capturing article pages that referenced other deals rather than standalone deal announcements. These are listed in Section 8 (Appendix) and excluded from all figures above. Announcement dates are missing for four named deals (JET Connectivity, Lentitek, Quantcore, Launchpad), so the precise date range cannot be established. Datable deals cluster between March and May 2026. This is not a single-week snapshot — the pipeline is catching up on deals announced over approximately five months.

## 6. Sector Heat Map

**Energy & Cleantech is the leading sector by capital.** Exergy3 (£10m), Earth Blox (£6m), Sisaltech (£620k), and — from the broader ledger — Carbogenics (£2.2m) represent over £18.8m in cleantech capital. The themes are consistent: industrial decarbonisation, nature-risk analytics, sustainable materials, carbon removal. Three Exergy3 co-investors came from overseas (France, Germany, Singapore), signalling that European climate-focused funds now treat Scottish companies as viable portfolio targets on par with continental alternatives.

**Deep Tech has the highest deal count** — five named deals (Quantcore, HonuWorx, Launchpad, JET Connectivity, and Exergy3 straddling cleantech/deep tech). The common thread is UK sovereign capability: quantum hardware manufacturing, subsea defence robotics, sovereign 5G, and AI assembly automation. This aligns with the broader UK government focus on strategic technologies. Deep tech deals are typically small at seed (sub-£1m for STAC and Tricapital) with occasional larger internationally-led outliers.

**Life Sciences has consistent activity but modest deal sizes.** Lentitek (£700k), Biocaptiva (£1.58m), and Kaly (cross-listed with agritech) sit in this category. The Edinburgh-Roslin corridor generates most of the Scottish life sciences deal flow captured here. EnteroBiotix (£19m, in the wider ledger) is a notable exception in terms of scale, led by the specialist Thairm Bio fund.

**Healthtech with AI** is present through Bioliberty's Lifehub platform. The Bioliberty, Entourage AI (ledger), and TileBio (ledger) deals all combine AI with clinical or health-monitoring applications — a theme that recurs independently of sector classification.

**Fintech is conspicuously absent** from both this run and the broader ledger. Given Edinburgh's historical identity as a fintech hub (Skyscanner, FreeAgent, Float), the absence is notable. It may reflect source coverage rather than a genuine gap — fintech deals often announce via press releases that require different sources to capture.

**Agritech / Blue Economy** is emerging as a niche with Kaly, Carbogenics (biochar, ledger), and the wider Tricapital thesis. Not yet a critical mass, but Scotland's coastline and land resources give it a credible structural basis.
