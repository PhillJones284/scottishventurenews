## 1. Executive Summary

**8 confirmed investment deals** (high or medium confidence) covering the period January–May 2026, with a combined estimated capital deployed of approximately **£31.6m**. One additional record (ePass) is held in the appendix — no investment has closed.

**Headline observations:**

- **Edinburgh dominates.** Seven of the eight confirmed deals involve Edinburgh-based companies. Glasgow accounts for one deal (Highway Data Systems). There is no confirmed activity in Aberdeen or other Scottish cities this period.
- **Cleantech is the standout sector.** Two of the three largest deals — Exergy3 (£10m) and Earth Blox (£6m) — are climate/energy plays, together accounting for more than half of all capital deployed this period. The trend reflects both investor appetite and the strength of University of Edinburgh spinout activity in the space.
- **Maven Capital Partners is the most active single fund manager** this period, appearing in two confirmed deals (Esk, Highway Data Systems) via the Investment Fund for Scotland. Combined with its appearance in the historical Nami Surgical deal, Maven is currently the most consistent institutional investor in the Scottish market.
- **Data caveat:** The Carbogenics figure ($3m / ~£2.2m) blends equity and grant funding; the pure equity component is unknown. Round types are undisclosed for three deals (Biocaptiva, Highway Data Systems, ePass).

### Exergy3 — Seed — £10m
**Sector:** Energy & Cleantech
**Location:** Edinburgh
**Lead investor:** Axeleo Capital
**Co-investors:** Bayern Capital, Kibo Invest, Scottish Enterprise Investment Fund, Zero Carbon Capital, Old College Capital
**Date:** 21 April 2026
**Sources:** [Edinburgh Innovations](https://edinburgh-innovations.ed.ac.uk/news/spinout-exergy3-raises-10m-to-decarbonise-industrial-heat)

Exergy3 is a University of Edinburgh spinout commercialising an ultra-high-temperature thermal energy storage system that converts surplus renewable electricity into industrial-grade heat up to 1,200°C. The technology addresses a real industrial gap — decarbonising high-temperature processes in sectors like distilling, cement, and steel — and has already been piloted at Annandale Distillery in one of the world's first low-carbon whisky productions. The round is notable for being led by a Paris-based European deeptech VC (Axeleo's Article 9 Green Tech Industry I fund) rather than a UK or Scottish investor, suggesting Edinburgh cleantech is attracting cross-border institutional interest.

## 6. Sector Heat Map

**Energy & Cleantech** is the standout sector this period, accounting for three deals and over half of all capital deployed (approximately £18.2m across Exergy3, Earth Blox, and Carbogenics). This is not a coincidence — it reflects the depth of University of Edinburgh IP in energy and climate science, and strong investor appetite for industrial decarbonisation and nature risk analytics globally. Both Exergy3 and Earth Blox have attracted international investors (Axeleo Capital from France; the European Space Agency) that are not active in other Scottish deals, suggesting the sector is gaining cross-border visibility.

**Life Sciences / Healthtech** accounts for two deals (Bioliberty and Biocaptiva) and approximately £9.3m. Both are Edinburgh University spinouts in early-to-mid stage development, both targeting US markets, and both backed by the same core syndicate (Archangels, Old College Capital, Scottish Enterprise). The healthtech ecosystem in Edinburgh appears to operate as a relatively tight network — the same investors appear repeatedly, which limits the capital pool but also suggests a reliable syndicate structure for companies that can get into it.

**Creative & Media and Property & Construction Tech** each account for one deal via Maven/IFS. These are less thematic and more opportunistic — companies with strong customer traction and commercial models that fit Maven's growth-stage mandate, regardless of sector. This is useful context for founders: Maven's sector is less important than stage and revenue profile.

**No activity** was captured in Fintech, SaaS, AgriTech, Space Tech, or AI/ML as standalone categories this period, though AI is woven into several deals (Bioliberty's AI-enabled robotics, Earth Blox's AI-powered satellite data analysis). The absence of standalone fintech and SaaS deals may reflect gaps in source coverage as much as actual market activity.
