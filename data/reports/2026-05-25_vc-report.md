# Scottish VC Investment Tracker — Weekly Intelligence Report
**Report date:** 25 May 2026  
**Period covered:** January–May 2026 (earliest deal: 29 Jan 2026; latest: 11 May 2026)

---

## 1. Executive Summary

**10 investments confirmed** across the period, totalling approximately **£22.8m** in capital deployed to Scottish companies. All records in this run are new to the ledger — this is the first run of the tracker.

**Key observations:**

1. **Deep tech is the standout theme.** Three deals in quantum hardware, photonics, and offshore wind inspection signal growing investor appetite for science-heavy, university-adjacent companies — particularly from Glasgow. Quantcore and Cnuic together attracted over £4.7m and a notably international investor set.

2. **Seed stage dominates by value.** Four seed-stage deals account for £15.3m of the £22.8m total — driven primarily by Exergy3's £10m raise, the largest deal in the dataset by a substantial margin. Early-stage activity is healthy in volume but capital is highly concentrated.

3. **Maven Capital Partners and STAC Invest are the most active deployers this period.** Maven made three separate investments across Glasgow and Edinburgh; STAC Invest backed three Glasgow-based companies through its accelerator programme. Scottish Enterprise participated in four deals, consistent with its co-investment mandate.

**Data caveats:** The date range spans approximately 3.5 months (late January to mid-May 2026), making period-on-period comparisons unavailable for this inaugural run. The ledger has no prior history to draw on. Two records carry medium rather than high confidence — see Section 8.

## 6. Sector Heat Map

**Energy & Cleantech** is the largest single sector by capital (£10.3m across two deals — Exergy3 and Airspection), driven almost entirely by Exergy3's £10m raise. The sector attracted the most international investor interest of any sector this period, with French, German, and UK climate VCs all participating in the Exergy3 round. Scotland's renewable energy infrastructure is generating genuine commercial spinout activity, not just grant-funded R&D.

**Deep Tech** is the most active sector by deal count (3 confirmed deals: Quantcore, Cnuic, plus Airspection which straddles Energy/Deep Tech). Glasgow is the centre of gravity — both Quantcore and Airspection are based there, as is STAC's accelerator. The quantum hardware angle (Quantcore) and photonics (Cnuic) represent genuinely novel hard-tech, not software labelled as deep tech. This is worth watching.

**Life Sciences and Healthtech** together account for 2 deals (BIOCAPTIVA, Nami Surgical) totalling £3.48m. Both are university spinouts (Edinburgh and Glasgow respectively). Edinburgh's Archangels and Old College Capital are the primary backers. The sector is active but not outsized relative to its historical prominence in the Scottish ecosystem.

**Property & Construction Tech** produced two small deals (SWURF £200k, Vuabl £222k), both sub-£250k angel/accelerator rounds. Not yet drawing institutional VC capital.

**Creative & Media** (Esk, £2.6m) and **Mobility & Transport** (Highway Data Systems, £1.25m) each have one deal. Neither is a historically prominent Scottish VC sector; both deals are IFS-backed via Maven rather than independent VC conviction.

**Absent sectors:** No deals in Fintech, AgriTech, Space Tech, or AI/ML as standalone categories. This may reflect source coverage gaps as much as market conditions.
