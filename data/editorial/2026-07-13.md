No new deals picked up by the agent for this week's Scottish Venture News, but it did highlight the recent £52m Series B investment in Wordsmith AI led by Highland Europe.

With all the changes going on in the world right now, if we want to be successful, we can't afford to be fighting the last war. We need to learn from what and who is successful right now and, dare I suggest it, try to figure out what will be successful next.

I find the success of Wordsmith really interesting for two reasons.

Firstly, they're an AI company, but they're also aimed at a specific market opportunity. That is, companies want to reduce spend on outside legal counsel.

What sets them apart from many of the AI startups that I've seen is that the AI part is treated like table stakes, which it is. The real discriminator is the ability to solve an articulated business problem that the ideal customer recognises in themselves.

The other thing that's interesting is that in all of the discussions that I've had with folks in the Scottish startup ecosystem over the past few months, nobody has mentioned them to me. People talk about Skyscanner and that gambling site I won't mention, but hardly anybody is talking about the current big success in progress.

The question that's puzzling me this morning is, 'Why not?'
