3. Deal Spotlight

Aveni — Growth — £12m

Lead investor: PXN Ventures · Co-investors: Puma Growth Partners, Lloyds Banking Group, Nationwide, Scottish Enterprise Investment Fund · Sector: AI & Machine Learning, Fintech · Location: Edinburgh

University of Edinburgh spinout Aveni builds AI compliance and conduct-risk tooling for financial services — specifically the infrastructure regulated firms need as they deploy AI agents in client-facing roles. Its Unified Assurance Platform monitors financial advice quality in real time; newer products (Agent Assure and Agent Approve) are designed to assess the behaviour of AI agents in regulated settings. The £12m Growth round was led by PXN Ventures, with Puma Growth Partners returning alongside Lloyds Banking Group and Nationwide as new strategic investors, and Scottish Enterprise also participating. The involvement of two of the UK's largest retail banks as co-investors — rather than simply customers — signals conviction that Aveni is building infrastructure they're already depending on. Announced 4 June; appeared in monitored sources this week.

Source: Edinburgh Innovations, Scottish Financial Review

Go Swag — Seed — $5m (~£3.7m)

Lead investor: Mercia Asset Management · Co-investors: Techstart Ventures · Sector: SaaS & Enterprise Software, corporate gifting · Location: Glasgow

Glasgow-based Go Swag runs a tech-enabled corporate gifting and branded merchandise platform, counting Meta, Apple, Netflix, and ElevenLabs among more than 1,000 client companies. The $5m Seed — led by Mercia, which manages the equity component of the Investment Fund for Scotland — brings total funding to $6.2m. Techstart Ventures, an active early-stage backer across the Scottish ecosystem, also participated. The combination of Mercia and Techstart suggests a deal with a deliberate Scottish growth mandate attached, despite the international client base. Announced 27 May; appeared in monitored sources this week.

Source: Crunchbase
